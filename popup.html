One-time setup

This helper signs you in inside a private window, and needs permission to see it. Firefox won’t let an add-on open its own settings page, so this part is manual (just once):

  1. Tap Copy “about:addons” below, paste it in the address bar, press Enter
  2. On “Dumb Down login helper,” click the ··· (three dots) → Manage
  3. Set “Run in Private Windows” to Allow
  4. Click the extension icon again

Why private? If the login is taken from your normal browser, Google signs your flip phone out about 30 minutes later.

Sign in privately

We’ll open a private window. Sign into your Google account there — the QR code then opens automatically.

Why private? A login taken from your normal browser gets signed out by Google about 30 minutes later. A private sign-in that you close afterwards keeps your flip phone linked.

Waiting for sign-in…

Finish signing into Google in the private window. The QR code opens on its own once you’re in — no need to click this icon again.

Stay on the page the sign-in lands on (the text-filled page is fine).
